Artificial Intelligence. Is it the future of cybersecurity?

AI, Articial Intelligence, Cybersecurity, Prevention, Detection, Cyber Threat

The future is now, and the importance of the role Artificial Intelligence (AI) plays in cybersecurity is substantial. As cyber threats and attacks intensify post pandemic, the traditional strategies seemingly fall behind the new and improved age of ransomware, malware and hacking attempts. Businesses and organizations are now in pursuit of efficient and effective ways to identify, prevent, counteract and respond to cyber threats. Utilizing software with artificial intelligence improves security measures by proactively protecting systems via flagging and alerting any activity with malicious intent. AI has three primary functions including prediction to identify vulnerabilities, detection by sending notifications to prevent an attack, and response to fight and stop attacks in real time. When programmed, AI software can detect and eliminate potential threats minus the need for an individual to intervene. Cybersecurity professionals now take numerous approaches to utilize the benefit of AI such as, machine learning-assisted hacking tools, machine learning-assisted malware analysis, and intrusion detection systems. Keeping up with technological changes is the key to preventing attacks as cybercriminals are upping their game. AI is now used across the spectrum from cybercriminals utilizing for their personal criminal activities, to cybersecurity professionals defending systems and networks against malicious attacks. So, is artificial intelligence the future of cybersecurity? It will play a crucial role in the future of cybersecurity but by no means is it perfect, yet. As cybercriminals become more sophisticated in attacks, it ^`^ys prominent to stay ahead by constantly innovating and adapting security measures. I personally don ^`^yt think relying solely on AI, without human intervention, to prevent or respond to attacks is a legitimate solution. As with anything, errors and false positives could cause obstacles or further damage. A combination of using AI learning algorithms and predictive analysis tools with human proficiency and knowledge, can build a strong defense against cyber threats.
